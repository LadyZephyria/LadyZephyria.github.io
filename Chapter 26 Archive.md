Zephyria stretched languidly within her stable that she had retreated into to lick her wounds and rest. Whatever the mortal's had done that had numbed her pain had long since faded leaving her sore and able to feel as her wounds pulled at their scabs, threatening to tear. She was careful to not stretch too far, but really wanted to get a good intense stretch in to loosen up her sore muscles. "*I wish I could go swimming again. My heart-flame might help me heal, but being at the bottom of that lake was miraculous.*" She thought to herself.

Stuck being forced to not only rest, but to wait on her companion's recovery, she carefully grabbed her enchanted took and laid down to begin reading it. "*I hope Conall is handling the treatment that healing order is giving him comfortably. I didn't see his hand very clearly, but that burn seemed painful. I'm grateful he cast that spell, I would have gotten hurt even worse if he didn't sacrifice his health to save me, again.*" She thought regretfully.

As she laid there contemplating, her chosen scooted and shuffled his gear over so that he could lean against her hind leg as he worked, occasionally itching himself. As she flipped it open to where she left off, Asher looked at the now open pages, then at her, with a slightly perplexed expression on his face.

"I had thought you had been reading that book for many hours by now, Zephyria. Are you re-reading some parts?" He said, with interest in his voice.

Zephyria looked at the book she had been reading. She was only a few chapters in and was beginning to work her way through the next one. "I don't know what you mean, little one. I'm reading at the same pace I always read. Should I be further along?"

Asher looked at her awkwardly, "Ah, uh. No, not at all. Its good to read at your own pace. I'm sorry I said anything."

Zephyria curled around herself and bonked him with her muzzle, "It sounds like your calling me a slow reader, little one. How far along would you be, if you had been reading it?" She said, self consciously.

Asher rubbed the back of his neck in embarrassment, then itched his arms again a little more vigorously. "A lot further in, probably. That said chapter six, right? I only read a little, but I finished the first two chapters in the store before I brought it to you and that only took me half an hour or so. But I had to do a lot of reading as a youth. I was a noble, remember? I had tutors and the like constantly forcing me to read complex texts, write letters, and the like. I'm not a good comparison."

"But, I'm still a slow reader? Even in comparison to the typical mortal that can read?" Zephyria said slightly sourly.

Asher nodded, still scratching his arms. "Damn I'm itchy for some reason." he mumbled before continuing. "I didn't mean to be rude, Zeph. But.. yea. You are. I thought you had read a lot, so I just assumed you would be like me. Its fine, really. Not like you or the book are going anywhere. That thing's enchanted to last an eternity and you'll live just as long. As long a your enjoying it, that's what matters."

Zephyria rested her muzzle on his lap as she curled around him slightly, "I have read a lot, my chosen. But, I am entirely self taught. I already told you much of what I stole was aimed at mortal hatchlings. In truth, I could not read even a little before I began stealing those books. It was a blessing really, to be able to teach myself to read. To my understanding it is a skill very few dragons have, as not even my mother learned to read in her long life."

Asher nudged his gear to the side and out of the way, while beginning to stroke the top of her muzzle. "I never considered that you would be entirely self taught. That's... That's incredibly impressive Zeph. Most mortals wouldn't be able to to do that. Not with just a random assortment of children's books, at least." He said gently. Zephyria purred slightly at his praise.

Asher scratched at his arms, and then swore slightly under his breath. "You know, I could help teach you to read better, when we have time. Not just read, but I can pass along all of the lessons I learned when it came to the topic. Themes, analysis, whatever."

Zephyria smiled slightly at the thought of that, but before she said anything she smelled blood. She lifted her head and sniffed, then looked down at her mortals arm and saw it bleeding slightly. Asher followed her gaze and looked startled. "Damn, I thought I felt a pinch. I've never cut myself scratching before, even with these new claws, that's really odd."

Zephyria looked closer, and then licked at the wound to wipe the blood way. Asher began to protest, then stopped as he saw a few gleaming Azure scales where his skin had once been. It was only a small patch, an inch across at most, but it still stunned the pair.

"They look just like mine, little dragon." Zephyria said, pridefully.

Asher nodded hesitantly. "Its strange, this feels more 'real' than the rest of the changes. Like this one is the beginning of something greater? I can't see my own eyes very often, sharper teeth is odd but still somewhat normal, and while the claws are unusual they aren't too different than many of the beast-kin we've met lately. But the scales... they just matter more to me, somehow." Asher said pensively.

"It may be because this is the first change that marks you as truly different. It marks you unmistakably as my chosen, little dragon. You may be hesitant now, but when you are fully clad in my scales and standing taller than ever before, you will look back and wonder how you could have ever been afraid, little one." Zephyria reassured Asher the best she could.

Asher nodded along with her words while he absently scratched at a few spots on his body, largely on his forearms and hands. "I just hope Conall likes them. He liked the eyes, at least." Asher mumbled. He looked between his now bloody claws and the half revealed patches of scales on his body. "As much as I want to clean my gear, I'm only going to get filthier if I don't take care of this mess right away. I'm going to go into the inn and use the washroom. Afterwards would you like me to help you learn to read better? At least until Conall returns from his visit to the healers?"

Zephyria purred slightly. "I would like that very much, little dragon. I look forward to your return and pray that Conall heals quickly. The burn on his hand looked unpleasant." She said as she opened her book once more, and began to slowly work through the pages while she waited.

Conall winced as the bandage wrapped around his left hand a little too tightly and he had to tap the member of the Order and get their attention. "That's a little tight, lass. Its a burn, not an open wound." He said gently.

It was Conall's turn to look embarrassed. "I understand, and I'm still really sorry about that. It took me too long to realize what it was I needed..." He trailed off uncomfortably. "I don't know if it helps, but I've had my eyes on Asher, the dragon's chosen. The feelings are mutual, too."

Two healers came in saying she was injured and they had the luxury of helping her, but they hardly mentioned her personality or the chosen himself! Conall took a moment to look over his bandaged hand. The bandages were lightly soaked in some kind of healing ointment he wasn't familiar with that was supposedly good for burns. There was some kind of pain killer mixed in too, he could feel his hand mostly fine but the horrible ache that had been following him since that fight was gone.

"They're a good pair. Good-natured. At least, as good-natured as a dragon can be. Zephyria can be a bit cold and aloof, but she cares deeply for those she lets into her life. And Asher... Asher's a very good man. Courageous, smart, caring. As a chosen, he's a bit wild at times but I find that attractive, honestly. I watched him tackle a man, and during the wrestle he ended choking the man out from behind and underneath, then drew his-" He cut himself off sharply before he could give the healer the gruesome details of their most recent fight, and looked at the her apologetically. "A normal burn from a flame? A few days to do most of the healing, then a week before your back to normal. Lighting burns? I'm unsure. Its a rare thing. The warlock that your patron fought today is in here as well, head to toe burns just barely on the skin. Whatever armor that warlock wore, or whatever spell she cast, it saved her from a terrible fate. Instead, she just has these dark trees all along her skin. Her fur hides most of it, but she's gone bald in some other spots and its incredibly visible. I've never even heard of something like it." Seana said.

Conall grimaced. "I've seen what she can do when someone isn't protected. As far as I can tell they die instantly, normally. A mercy given the state of them afterwards." He said. Seana's eyes widened and then she shook her head dismissively.

"I don't want to know." She said matter-of-factly, somewhat disturbed. "Your burns-" She refocused the conversation with a smile that was only a little forced. "-they should heal in a week or two, but its hard to say exactly how long. The burn looked like a spell gone wrong, so don't do that again." She said, her teasing tone returning near the end.

Conall rolled his eyes. "Thank you, Seana. I'll be sure not to use magic as a warlock." He snarked. "But, seriously, Seana. Thank you for your help. I think its about time I got back to my patron and made sure she's doing alright. At least make sure she's not getting in any more trouble."

Seana smiled genuinely this time. "You don't need to pretend to be thinking about your patron, Conny, I know you well enough to know who's really on your thoughts. Go to your man, I'm sure you miss him already." She giggled a little bit. "You always were the clingy type." She said with a knowing and affectionate look in her eyes.
